Google reviews matter for med spas because clients often research trust signals before booking a facial, injectable consultation, laser appointment, or skin treatment.

The hard part is not knowing that reviews matter. The hard part is asking consistently without sounding awkward, pushy, or risky.

Policy-safe reminder: ask real clients for honest feedback. Do not ask only satisfied clients, offer incentives, suggest a star rating, or influence what they write.

Policy-Safe Review Request Basics

Before using any template, keep the request simple and honest.

Do

  • Ask real clients for honest feedback.
  • Use the same general workflow across clients.
  • Send the request after a real appointment.
  • Make the message easy to ignore if the client does not want to review.
  • Include your Google review link.

Avoid

  • Asking only clients you believe are happy.
  • Offering discounts, gifts, or rewards for reviews.
  • Asking for a specific star rating.
  • Writing the review for the client.
  • Pressuring clients to mention specific services or results.

When To Send The Request

  1. Send the review request after the appointment is complete.
  2. Use your normal follow-up channel, such as SMS or email.
  3. Track that the request was sent.
  4. Do not repeatedly chase the client.
  5. Reply to new reviews on a regular weekly schedule.

Simple Weekly Review Workflow

Monday: review the previous week's completed appointments and send requests where appropriate.

Wednesday: reply to new Google reviews.

Friday: check whether any review requests were missed and update the tracker.
