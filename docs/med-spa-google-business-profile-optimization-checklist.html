The 20-point checklist

Core identity (do these first)

  1. Pick the right primary category. "Medical Spa" if you offer injectables or prescription treatments and have a physician medical director. "Beauty Salon" or "Skin Care Clinic" otherwise. Wrong category quietly tanks ranking for the searches you actually want.
  2. Add 3 to 5 secondary categories. Examples: "Skin Care Clinic," "Laser Hair Removal Service," "Massage Spa." Picking too many dilutes the signal; 3 to 5 is the sweet spot.
  3. Business name matches signage and legal name. No keyword-stuffing ("Bella Med Spa - Botox - Filler - Best in Dallas"). Google penalizes this and competitors can flag the listing.
  4. NAP consistency. Name, address, phone match exactly across your website, Yelp, Facebook, Apple Maps, and Google. Even a "St." vs "Street" mismatch creates a ranking drag.

Service surface

  1. List every service individually. Use the Services section to add each treatment as a separate item with its own short description. Most spas list 4 to 6 services; the strong ones list 25 to 40.
  2. Use starting-at price ranges, not exact prices. "Starting at $300" beats "$300" because it allows for consultation variance and reduces price-objection no-shows.
  3. Write a real description. 600 to 750 characters. Mention 2 to 3 top services, your neighborhood or city, and one differentiator (medical director's background, specific certification). Avoid stuffing.

Visuals

  1. Upload 20 to 40 photos. Real photos of the space, the staff (with consent), products, and waiting area. Stock photos are obvious and tank trust. Phone photos taken in natural light beat stock every time.
  2. Geotag photos before upload. Modern phones do this automatically when location permissions are on. Geotagged photos compound the local signal.
  3. Update one photo per week. Photo recency is a real freshness signal. A spa that hasn't posted a photo in 8 months looks dormant to Google.
  4. Skip before-and-after photos in your main feed. Google can flag medical before-and-afters as a policy issue. Keep clinical before-and-afters on your website where you control disclosure.

Hours, attributes, and Q&A

  1. Hours match reality and special hours are set for holidays. Wrong holiday hours generate negative reviews from frustrated walk-ups. Set special hours for every federal holiday and any spa-specific closure.
  2. Fill in every applicable attribute. Women-owned, LGBTQ+ friendly, wheelchair accessible parking, free Wi-Fi, gift cards available. Attributes are filter facets that route qualified searchers to you.
  3. Seed the Q&A section yourself. Post the 6 to 10 most common questions and answer them. Otherwise random users post them and you have less control over the framing.

Activity signals

  1. Post one Google Post per week. Posts decay after 7 days. Weekly posts keep the listing active. Use the "offer" or "update" type; skip "event" unless you have a real event.
  2. Reply to every review within 72 hours. Reply density is a small but consistent ranking signal. Calm, brief, HIPAA-safe replies on every review.
  3. Update your services and description quarterly. Freshness compounds. A profile last edited 14 months ago reads as abandoned to Google.

What is overrated and what is underrated

Overrated (still do them, but not the priority)

  • Adding 50 services. After 25 to 40, returns flatten.
  • Daily Google Posts. Weekly is enough.
  • Long descriptions. 600 to 750 characters beats 1500.
  • Booking link integrations. Useful but not a ranking factor.

Underrated (most spas skip these and pay for it)

  • Q&A seeding. Most profiles have zero owner-posted Q&A.
  • Photo recency. Quietly the strongest freshness signal.
  • Reply density. Most spas reply to half their reviews. Reply to all.
  • Secondary categories. Most spas have one. Three to five is the right answer.
  • Attribute completion. Most spas fill in 4 attributes; there are 30+ applicable.

Frequently asked questions

How long does optimization take?

First-pass on an existing profile: 90 to 120 minutes. Full setup from scratch: 3 to 4 hours. Weekly maintenance: about 30 minutes.

Which category: medical spa or beauty salon?

Medical Spa if you have a physician medical director and offer prescription/injectable treatments. Otherwise Beauty Salon or Skin Care Clinic. Wrong category tanks ranking for medical-spa search terms.

Should I list prices?

Optional. "Starting at" ranges work better than exact prices.

Does the description affect SEO?

Yes, modestly. Use natural language, mention top services and city, avoid stuffing.

How do reviews tie in?

Reviews are the largest local ranking factor owners control. Profile optimization is the floor; reviews are the engine.

The honest bottom line

Profile optimization is a one-time-plus-maintenance task. Reviews are a forever task.
