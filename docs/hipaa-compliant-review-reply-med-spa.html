HIPAA compliant review replies for med spas.

This is not legal advice. It is a practical guide. If your med spa handles complex cases or has a compliance officer, run replies past them.

Why this matters

HIPAA covers protected health information. A Google review reply is public forever. If your reply confirms that someone was a patient or what care they received, you are confirming protected health information in public.

The safest rule: assume every public reply is read by a HIPAA auditor.

What you cannot do in a public reply

  • Confirm the reviewer was a patient at your med spa.
  • Confirm what treatment, procedure, or service they received.
  • Reference dates of visits, appointment notes, or follow-up plans.
  • Reference health history, allergies, conditions, or medications.
  • Name the provider who treated them.
  • Discuss billing or insurance specifics tied to their record.

What you can do

  • Thank them generally for the feedback.
  • Speak to your team's values and standards in general terms.
  • Invite a private conversation.
  • Say "we cannot discuss specifics publicly" and stop there.
  • Use your med spa's name.

The 6 line safe reply formula

  1. Hi [first name from the review, if given].
  2. Thank you for taking the time to share this.
  3. [One short line acknowledging the feeling, not the fact.]
  4. Because privacy matters, we cannot discuss specifics publicly.
  5. Please reach out to us at [Phone or Email] so we can learn more.
  6. Sincerely, the team at [Med Spa Name].

Two safe examples

Positive review reply (still privacy-aware)

Thank you so much for sharing this. We are grateful you trusted [Med Spa Name] with your visit, and we are glad the experience felt like the calm, professional one we work hard to provide. We look forward to welcoming you back. Sincerely, the team at [Med Spa Name].

Why it is safe: nothing in the reply confirms the specific service. Just thanks, values, invitation back.

Negative review reply (privacy-safe)

Hi [name]. Thank you for taking the time to share this. We are sorry your visit did not meet expectations. Because privacy matters, we cannot discuss specifics publicly. Please contact us at [Phone or Email] so our team can learn more. Sincerely, the team at [Med Spa Name].

Three classic mistakes to avoid

  1. "We have no record of you as a client." This confirms the absence of a relationship, which is still information about a specific person. Stay general.
  2. "Per your consent form..." You just confirmed a consent form exists for that person. Stay general.
  3. "Your [specific treatment] outcome can vary..." You just confirmed the treatment. Use "results from any aesthetic service can vary."

Frequently asked questions

Does HIPAA apply to my med spa's Google reviews?

If your med spa provides medical services and bills for them, you are likely a HIPAA covered entity. Even non-medical aesthetic services should follow the same conservative public communication rules.

Can a patient share their own treatment info in a review?

Yes. A patient can share whatever they want about their own care. You still cannot confirm or expand on it in a public reply.

Can I say "we don't have a record of you as a client"?

No. That confirms the absence of a relationship, which is still information about a specific person. Keep replies general.

What is a safe phrase to invite a private conversation?

"Because privacy matters, we cannot discuss specifics publicly. Please contact us at [Phone or Email] so we can learn more."
