Refinance signing prep checklist.

What is typically in a conventional refi package, the pitfalls that void scans, the RTC handling rule that gets mixed up most, and the funding question you should not answer at the table.

A conventional rate-and-term refinance on a primary residence is the most common signing in a working NSA's calendar. Right of Rescission applies, scanbacks are usually due within two hours, and one wrong date on the Notice of Right to Cancel voids the package.

This is the prep card we use. Source files were rebuilt on 2026-05-11 after two working signing agents corrected the RTC handling and the signer-question referral language. Both corrections are baked into the version below.

Not legal advice. Notary regulations and refinance practices vary by state and lender. This page documents general operational patterns observed in conventional refi signings. Consult your state Secretary of State or commissioning authority for state-specific rules, and your hiring title company for package-specific instructions.

What is typically in the package

  • Note. Signed, not notarized.
  • Deed of Trust or Mortgage. Notarized, every borrower on title.
  • Closing Disclosure. Signed, not notarized.
  • Notice of Right to Cancel. Each borrower signs two copies that go back to title; each borrower keeps two UNSIGNED copies.
  • Signature and Name Affidavit. Notarized.
  • Occupancy Affidavit. Notarized.
  • Compliance Agreement. Notarized.
  • Errors and Omissions / Correction Agreement. Signed.
  • W-9. Signed.
  • First Payment Letter. Acknowledgment only.
  • Patriot Act and Borrower ID Verification. Notary fills the ID section.
  • Initial Escrow Disclosure. Signed.

Common pitfalls

Right of Rescission date math. Count three business days starting the day AFTER signing. Exclude Sundays and federal holidays. Wrong dates void the package.
Non-borrowing spouse signatures. In a community-property or dower state, the non-borrowing spouse may still sign the Deed of Trust and the Right to Cancel only. Confirm with the title company before signing.
AKA handling on the Name Affidavit. The Name Affidavit lists every AKA the title company pulled. The borrower signs each variation exactly as printed. For any AKA the signer does not go by, write "not known as" next to it.
Funding versus signing. Funding is not the day of signing. Borrowers expect their money the same day. Set the expectation early: the Disbursement Date follows rescission, not signing.
RTC handling. Each borrower signs two copies of the Notice of Right to Cancel that go BACK to the title company in the scanback or overnight package. Each borrower keeps two UNSIGNED copies for their records. Mixing this up is the most common error in the package.

Scanback expectations

Most title companies want a full scanback the same day, often within two hours of signing. Originals get overnighted by the next business day via the prepaid label included in the package. Confirm the scanback list with the title company before leaving the table; do not assume the standard set covers their request.

Common signer questions and the right referral

Signing agents are not licensed to give loan advice. Refer all funding, loan term, and payoff questions to the Loan Officer, even when the answer seems obvious. This protects you and protects the package.

  • "When do I get my money?" Refer to the Loan Officer. Do not quote a specific funding date.
  • "Why is my payoff higher than my balance?" Refer to the Loan Officer. Do not explain per diem, fees, or interest accrual yourself.
  • "Can I cancel?" Yes. Direct the borrower to sign the Right to Cancel before midnight of the rescission deadline. Do not opine on whether they should.
  • "Why is the rate different from the lock?" Refer to the Loan Officer.

For ink color, follow lender instructions. Most refi packages want blue ink, but a few title companies still require black.

Do NOT notarize

  • The Closing Disclosure.
  • The Note.
  • The Notice of Right to Cancel.
  • The First Payment Letter.
  • Lender or settlement agent instructions.
  • Any document without a notarial certificate already printed.

Quick stats for a typical refi

  • Signatures: 90 to 130.
  • Initials: 40 to 70.
  • Pages: 100 to 150.
  • Duration at the table: 45 to 75 minutes.
