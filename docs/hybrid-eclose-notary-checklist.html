Hybrid eClose signing checklist.

Most of the package is already eSigned in the borrower portal. You handle the wet-sign and the notarized pieces. Smaller package, tighter scanback, same attention to detail. Here is the prep card we use.

Hybrid eClose. Wet portion only. 20 to 40 minutes.

Smaller package than a full wet close, but the notarial requirements are the same. Common pitfall: the eSign portal is not complete when you arrive.

A hybrid eClose splits the loan package into two halves. Most documents get eSigned by the borrower in a portal in the days before closing. The notary handles only the wet-sign and notarized portion at the table, typically 20 to 40 minutes.

The wet portion is small but the variables shift. Different lenders draw the wet line in different places. Scanback windows are tighter. Borrowers walk in thinking they already finished. Here is what to check before, during, and after.

Not legal advice. eClose practices and state notarial requirements vary. Consult your state's commissioning authority for eNotarization rules in your jurisdiction and your hiring title company for package-specific instructions.

What is typically in the wet portion

The wet split varies by lender. The standard set of wet-signed documents in a hybrid refi or purchase usually includes:

  • Deed of Trust or Mortgage. Notarized.
  • Note. Often wet-signed, occasionally an eNote.
  • Signature and Name Affidavit. Notarized.
  • Occupancy Affidavit. Notarized.
  • Compliance Agreement. Notarized.
  • Notice of Right to Cancel if this is a refi. Each borrower signs two copies that go back to the title company in the scanback or overnight package, and each borrower keeps two UNSIGNED copies for their records.
  • Patriot Act and Borrower ID Verification.
  • State-required wet-sign documents.
  • Closing Disclosure acknowledgment. Sometimes wet, often pre-signed in the portal.

Common pitfalls

Confirm the hybrid split before the appointment. Call or message the title company and confirm exactly which documents are wet versus eSigned. Lenders are not consistent. The CD might be eSigned at one lender and wet at the next.
Verify the eSign portion is complete. Before signing the wet docs, confirm with the title company that the borrower finished the eSign portal. A half-finished portal blocks funding even if your wet portion is perfect.
Reset borrower expectations on arrival. Borrowers often think they already finished everything in the portal. Tell them up front: there is still a 15 to 30 minute wet-sign portion for documents that cannot be eSigned in most states.
Check Note format BEFORE the borrower signs. If the Note was eSigned in the portal, do NOT have the borrower also sign a paper Note. Two signed Notes create a legal mismatch. Confirm with the title company which version is in the wet package.
Same RTC rule as a full wet refi. Each borrower signs two RTC copies that go back to title. Each borrower keeps two UNSIGNED copies. The hybrid format does not change this.
Smaller package, same scanback urgency. Fewer pages means less time at the table, but the title company still wants the wet pieces back fast. Often 30 to 60 minutes after signing.

Scanback expectations

Hybrid eClose scanbacks are usually faster than full wet closes because the eSign portion is already complete and funding waits only on the wet pieces. Plan to scan from the car or a nearby location with reliable upload. Originals overnight via the prepaid label by the next business day. Confirm the scanback list with the title company before leaving the table; do not assume the standard set covers their request.

Common signer questions

  • "I already signed everything online, why are you here?" The notarized documents cannot be eSigned in most states. Brief explanation, then move on.
  • "When do I get my money? When does the deal record?" Refer to the Loan Officer and the title company. Do not quote specific funding or recording dates.
  • "Is this legal?" Yes. Hybrid eClose is a standard format used by most major lenders.
  • "Why are we even doing this in person?" State law on notarial certificates. Polite, brief answer, then keep moving.

Do NOT notarize

  • eSigned documents. They are already complete in the portal.
  • The Closing Disclosure.
  • The Note unless the package specifies a wet Note.
  • The Notice of Right to Cancel.
  • Lender or settlement agent instructions.
  • Any informational acknowledgment without a notarial certificate already printed.

Quick stats for a typical hybrid eClose

  • Signatures: 15 to 40 (wet portion only).
  • Initials: 5 to 20 (wet portion only).
  • Pages: 20 to 60 (wet portion only).
  • Duration at the table: 20 to 40 minutes.
