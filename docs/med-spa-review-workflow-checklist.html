Most med spas do not need a complicated review system. They need a repeatable habit: know who to ask, ask at the right moment, follow up politely, and reply without exposing private client details.

This checklist is built for owners, managers, and front-desk teams who want a clean Google review routine that fits into a busy week.

Policy-safe reminder: ask real clients for honest feedback. Do not review gate, pre-screen for happy clients only, pressure clients, offer incentives for reviews, or ask for a specific star rating.

The 30-Minute Weekly Review Workflow

1. Pull last week’s completed appointments. Look for clients who had a normal completed visit and are appropriate to contact under your communication policies.
2. Mark the request channel. Choose SMS, email, or phone follow-up based on the client’s allowed contact preferences and what your team already uses.
3. Send one honest-feedback request. Keep the message short. Thank them for visiting, ask for honest feedback, and include the direct Google review link.
4. Schedule one gentle follow-up. If your policy allows it, follow up once after a few days. Skip repeated nudges. Nobody books injectables because a brand became a notification goblin.
5. Reply to new reviews. Thank positive reviewers, stay calm with concerns, and avoid confirming treatment details in public replies.
6. Record what happened. Track who was asked, when, channel used, review status, reply status, and next action so nothing lives only in someone’s memory.

Simple Weekly Tracker Columns

If you are building this in a spreadsheet, start with these columns:

  • Client first name or internal initials
  • Visit date
  • Service category, kept broad
  • Request channel: SMS, email, phone, or none
  • Request sent date
  • Follow-up date
  • Review status: not requested, requested, reviewed, no response
  • Reply status: not needed, needs reply, replied
  • Next action owner

Copy-And-Paste Review Request

Use this as a starting point, then adjust the tone for your med spa.

Hi [First Name], thank you for visiting [Med Spa Name]. If you have a minute, we would be grateful for your honest feedback on Google: [Review Link] Your review helps local clients learn more about our team. Thank you again.

What To Avoid

  • Do not ask only visibly happy clients for public reviews.
  • Do not route unhappy clients to private feedback while routing happy clients to Google.
  • Do not offer discounts, loyalty points, free treatments, giveaways, or perks for reviews.
  • Do not ask staff to write reviews or ask clients for specific wording.
  • Do not mention protected or sensitive treatment details in public replies.

Front Desk Version

For a front-desk handoff, keep the habit tiny:

  1. Every Monday, open last week’s completed visits.
  2. Send approved review request messages to eligible clients.
  3. Every Thursday, check for new reviews and mark reply status.
  4. Reply using approved templates or escalate sensitive reviews to the manager.
  5. Update the tracker before closing the loop.
