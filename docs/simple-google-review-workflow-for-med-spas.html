Med spa reputation workflow

A simple Google review workflow for med spas that does not feel pushy.

Med spas do not usually have a review problem because every client is unhappy. They have a review workflow problem.

Happy clients leave without being asked at the right moment. Front desk teams get busy. Review replies wait until someone remembers. A bad review arrives, everyone panics, and then the clinic tries to fix reputation in crisis mode.

That is backwards. The review system should be boring, repeatable, and privacy-aware.

The real moment to ask

Most clinics wait too long. If a client says something positive at checkout, after a follow-up, or after a visible treatment milestone, that is the moment to ask. Three days later, the emotional peak has passed and the client has moved on.

The ask does not need to be dramatic:

I'm so glad you had a good experience today. Reviews really help local clients find us. Would you be open to leaving a quick Google review?

Then make it easy: QR code, direct review link, or a short text. Do not offer discounts or perks in exchange for reviews. It can create policy risk, and it can make the review profile feel less trustworthy.

The weekly review rhythm

  1. Check new Google reviews twice per week.
  2. Mark which reviews need a reply.
  3. Reply with short, calm, privacy-aware language.
  4. Track who was asked for a review and when.
  5. Ask happy repeat clients first.
  6. Review the numbers once per week: requests sent, reviews received, unanswered reviews, and service-recovery issues.

What to say in replies

Med spas need to be more careful than ordinary local businesses. A review reply should not confirm treatment details, medical history, appointment specifics, or anything private.

Thank you for your kind review. We appreciate you choosing our clinic and are grateful you took the time to share your experience.
Thank you for sharing your feedback. We are sorry to hear that your experience did not meet expectations. Because privacy matters, we cannot discuss appointment details publicly, but we would appreciate the opportunity to learn more. Please contact our team directly so we can review your concerns.

The goal is not to win an argument in public. The goal is to show future clients that the clinic is calm, professional, and attentive.

The front desk version

If your front desk is already overloaded, do not hand them a giant reputation project. Give them a tiny checklist:

  • Ask happy clients at checkout.
  • Send the direct review link.
  • Log the ask in a simple tracker.
  • Flag any negative review for owner or manager review.
  • Use approved reply templates instead of improvising every time.

That turns review management from "someone should do this" into a small operating habit.
