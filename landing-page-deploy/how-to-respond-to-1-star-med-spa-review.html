Reputation playbook - 1 star reviews

How to respond to a 1 star med spa review.

A 7 step process and 7 ready-to-copy reply examples for the most common 1 star complaints at a med spa. Privacy-safe wording. No defensive tone.

A 1 star Google review is read more carefully than any other review on your profile. Future clients scroll past the 5 star reviews to find the worst one and judge your business by how you handled it.

That means a 1 star review is not the end of trust. It is a chance to show calm.

Privacy reminder: never confirm a specific treatment, medical history, diagnosis, appointment time, or provider name in a public reply. Move details to a private channel.

The 7 step process

  1. Wait 24 hours. Defensive replies happen in the first hour. Give yourself a business day.
  2. Read the review carefully. What is the actual complaint? Cost, result, wait time, staff tone, hygiene, booking, or refund?
  3. Pick one template. Match the template to the complaint type. Do not mix.
  4. Strip private details. Remove anything that confirms what happened in the room.
  5. Move the conversation private. Always invite a call or email.
  6. Second set of eyes. Ask a teammate to read it. Defensive tone is hard to see in your own writing.
  7. Publish and log it. Mark the review as handled in your tracker.

Reply example 1: complaint about cost or pricing

The reviewer says they felt the service was overpriced or they were charged more than expected.

Thank you for sharing this feedback. We work hard to be transparent about pricing, and we are sorry your experience did not reflect that. We would like to understand what happened. Please reach out at [Phone or Email] so our team can look into the details with you directly.

Reply example 2: complaint about results

The reviewer is unhappy with how a treatment turned out.

Thank you for your honest feedback. Results from any aesthetic service can vary from person to person, and we take it seriously when an experience does not meet expectations. Because privacy matters, we cannot discuss specifics publicly. We would appreciate the chance to speak with you at [Phone or Email].

Reply example 3: complaint about wait time or scheduling

The reviewer felt rushed, kept waiting, or had a booking issue.

Thank you for taking the time to share this. We are sorry your visit did not match the calm, on-time experience we want every client to have. We are reviewing our scheduling process this month and your feedback helps. If you would like to share more, please contact us at [Phone or Email].

Reply example 4: complaint about staff tone

The reviewer felt a staff member was rude, cold, or dismissive.

Thank you for letting us know how this visit felt. We hold our team to a warm, professional standard, and we are sorry if your experience did not match that. We would like to learn more so we can address this internally. Please reach out at [Phone or Email].

Reply example 5: complaint about hygiene or cleanliness

This category is serious. Reply quickly and move private fast.

Thank you for raising this. Cleanliness and safety are not negotiable in our med spa, and any concern in this area gets a direct review from our leadership team. Please contact us at [Phone or Email] so we can look into your specific visit right away.

Reply example 6: complaint about a refund or billing dispute

The reviewer says they were promised a refund or were charged incorrectly.

Thank you for bringing this to our attention. Billing concerns are best handled directly so we can review records together. Please reach out to [Phone or Email] and our team will respond within one business day.

Reply example 7: vague or emotional complaint

The reviewer is upset but does not clearly state what went wrong.

Thank you for your feedback. We are sorry your visit did not meet expectations. We would like to understand more so we can do better. If you are open to it, please contact us at [Phone or Email].

What not to do in a 1 star reply

  • Do not argue point by point.
  • Do not confirm or deny what happened in the treatment room.
  • Do not promise a specific outcome or refund publicly.
  • Do not call the reviewer dishonest, even if you suspect it.
  • Do not copy the same reply onto every negative review.

Frequently asked questions

Should I respond to every 1 star review?

Yes. Silence reads as guilt or absence. A calm public reply protects trust with the next future client reading your profile.

Can I ask Google to remove a 1 star review?

Only if it violates Google's policy. Reply publicly first, then flag if it qualifies as fake, off-topic, or from someone who was never a client.

Should I offer a refund in the public reply?

No. Offers belong in private conversation. A public refund offer can read as buying silence.

What if the review names a specific provider?

Do not confirm or deny that provider treated the reviewer. Keep it general and move details private.

Is it safe to mention HIPAA in the reply?

You can say privacy or confidentiality prevents you from discussing specifics. You do not have to name HIPAA. Less jargon reads warmer.
