Bad review playbook

What to say when a patient writes a bad review.

A short, practical playbook for med spas. The 4 part reply formula. 6 example replies. Plain language only.

If you want a printable cheat sheet to keep at the front desk, the $10 swipe file has it.

A bad review feels personal. It is not. It is a public artifact future clients will read. Your job is to make sure the next person who reads it feels like your team is calm, careful, and reasonable.

Reminder: a med spa reply has to be privacy-safe. Do not confirm what treatment happened, when, with whom, or why.

The 4 part reply formula

  1. Thank. Open with a short thanks for the feedback. No flattery.
  2. Acknowledge. Name the feeling, not the fact. "We are sorry your visit did not meet expectations."
  3. Privacy line. "Because privacy matters, we cannot discuss specifics publicly."
  4. Private invite. "Please contact us at [Phone or Email] so we can learn more."

Two to four sentences total. That is the whole reply. Anything longer reads defensive.

Example 1: review says the result was bad

Thank you for your honest feedback. Results from any aesthetic service can vary from person to person, and we take it seriously when an experience does not meet expectations. Because privacy matters, we cannot discuss specifics publicly. Please contact us at [Phone or Email] so our team can speak with you directly.

Example 2: review says staff was rude

Thank you for letting us know how your visit felt. Our team is trained to give every client a calm, professional experience, and we are sorry yours did not match that. Please reach out to [Phone or Email] so we can learn more and address it internally.

Example 3: review says the cost was unfair

Thank you for sharing this feedback. We do our best to be clear about pricing, and we are sorry your visit did not reflect that. Please contact us at [Phone or Email] so our team can review the details with you.

Example 4: review says they were rushed

Thank you for your feedback. Every client should leave feeling heard, not hurried. We are sorry your visit did not reflect that. We would appreciate the chance to talk directly at [Phone or Email].

Example 5: review is emotional and unclear

Thank you for taking the time to share this. We are sorry your visit was not what you hoped for. We would like to understand more. Please reach out to [Phone or Email].

Example 6: review accuses staff of something serious

For anything that touches safety, hygiene, or harm, keep the public reply very short and move it private fast.

Thank you for raising this. Concerns like this go directly to our leadership team. Please contact us at [Phone or Email] so we can review your visit right away.

What never to say

  • "That is not what happened."
  • "We have no record of you as a client." (Even if true.)
  • "You signed the consent form, so..."
  • "We will be removing this review." (You probably cannot, and it sounds threatening.)
  • "Please update your review when we resolve this." (Reads as buying a higher rating.)

Frequently asked questions

What is the worst thing to say in a bad review reply?

Anything that confirms a specific treatment, diagnosis, or appointment detail. Also anything defensive, sarcastic, or accusing the reviewer of lying.

Can a bad review hurt my Google ranking?

One or two bad reviews on a profile with steady recent activity rarely hurt ranking. A long stretch with no replies and no new reviews hurts more.

Should I respond myself or have a manager respond?

Use a consistent signature like "The team at [Med Spa Name]" so replies look professional and not personal. Pick one person per week to draft and have a second read.
